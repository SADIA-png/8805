Coolie Begar Movement

Mass protest against forced unpaid labour

Why it Matters: Turned social grievance into organized public politics., A key landmark in anti-colonial and rights-based mobilization in Kumaon., Remembered as a major moment of dignity and resistance. (सामाजिक शिकायत को संगठित सार्वजनिक राजनीति में बदल दिया।, कुमाऊँ में उपनिवेशवाद विरोधी और अधिकार-आधारित लामबंदी में एक महत्वपूर्ण मील का पत्थर।, गरिमा और प्रतिरोध के एक प्रमुख क्षण के रूप में याद किया जाता है।)

The movement was led by Hargovind Pant and Badri Datt Pandey, who were later awarded the titles of 'Jannayak' and 'Kumaon Kesari', respectively, for their efforts. The movement aimed to address the grievances of the local population against the colonial begar system, which mandated unpaid forced labor from locals to transport officials' baggage, provide porters (coolies), and furnish other services without remuneration or adequate notice. The Coolie-Begar movement was sparked during the Uttarayani festival in January 1921. On this occasion, a large gathering of villagers took place at the confluence of the Saryu River and Gomti River, where they collectively protested against the unfair system. The people from different villages came together to demand better treatment and compensation for their labor, which was a significant departure from the traditional practices. The colonial administration had codified the begar system under British rule, which exploited the local population by forcing them to work without pay or fair compensation. This created socio-economic grievances among the villagers, who were already burdened with the responsibility of providing services to the governing classes. The movement highlighted the need for fair labor practices and better treatment of the common man. The Coolie-Begar movement was a pioneering effort in addressing the grievances of the local population against colonial exploitation. It marked an important milestone in the struggle for social justice and equality in India. Although the movement ultimately failed to achieve all its goals, it laid the foundation for future movements and struggles for human rights in the country.
