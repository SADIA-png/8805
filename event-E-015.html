The Askot-Arakot Yatra is a historic and unique journey that takes place every 10 years, where participants embark on a 45-day foot march through the western Himalayas to revive and restore lost ties between communities. The sixth edition of this yatra was flagged off on May 25, 2024, from Askot village in Pithoragarh district, Uttarakhand, and will conclude in Arakot village, Uttarkashi. The journey began with a group of passionate students and activists in 1974, who aimed to understand life in the remote areas of the Himalayas. Since then, this decade-long Himalayan journey has become an annual tradition, where participants from across India come together to walk through the picturesque landscapes of the western Himalayas. The yatra is not just a trekking adventure but also an opportunity for participants to learn about the culture, history, and traditions of the communities they visit along the way. The Askot-Arakot Yatra has become an iconic symbol of unity and solidarity among communities in the Himalayas. Over the years, the journey has been led by several notable leaders, including Gp Capt Achchyut Kumar Shekhar Pathak, who will lead the 6th edition of this yatra. The expedition aims to promote cultural understanding, exchange ideas, and strengthen bonds between communities, while also raising awareness about environmental conservation and social issues affecting the Himalayan region. The journey is carefully planned to ensure that participants experience a mix of scenic beauty, diverse cultures, and engaging experiences. Along the way, participants will have opportunities to engage with local communities, participate in cultural programs, and learn about traditional practices and customs. The yatra also includes educational sessions on environmental conservation, social issues, and community development. The Askot-Arakot Yatra is a testament to the power of unity and solidarity among communities in the Himalayas. As it continues to evolve and grow in popularity, this unique journey remains an essential part of India's cultural heritage, promoting cross-cultural understanding and exchange while celebrating the rich diversity of the Himalayan region.
