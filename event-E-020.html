The construction of the Tehri Dam in India's Uttarakhand state has been marred by decades of intense opposition from various stakeholders. One of the primary concerns was the massive displacement of around 100,000 people to make way for the dam, which was built on the Bhagirathi River. The affected communities were largely indigenous and rural, whose livelihoods were severely impacted by the project. Many families had to leave their homes, farms, and ancestral lands, leading to significant social and economic disruption. Another major criticism leveled against the Tehri Dam was its environmental impact. The dam's construction altered the natural flow of the Bhagirathi River, affecting the local ecosystem and wildlife habitats. The construction of a massive reservoir also raised concerns about water pollution, as silt and sediment from the river would accumulate in the reservoir, posing a threat to aquatic life. Seismic risks were another major concern surrounding the Tehri Dam. The region is prone to earthquakes due to its location in the Himalayas, making it a geologically unstable zone. The high risk of seismic activity posed significant concerns for the dam's structural integrity and safety, leading many experts to question the project's viability. Environmental organizations also argued that inadequate environmental impact assessments (EIA) were conducted before the dam's construction began. This lack of transparency and public participation raised questions about the project's social and environmental accountability. Critics claimed that alternative solutions, such as smaller dams or renewable energy projects, could have been explored to achieve similar development goals without sacrificing so much of the environment. The Tehri Dam controversy has had a lasting impact on the region, with ongoing debates about its legacy and long-term effects. The project's history serves as a reminder of the complex interplay between economic development, environmental sustainability, and social justice in large-scale infrastructure projects.
